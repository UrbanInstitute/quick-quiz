Utilitarian Ethics

Will you pull the lever?

A form of consequentialism, utilitarianism is generally held to be the view that the morally right action is the action that produces the most good.

Stoic Ethics

The Ancient Art of Not Caring

A philosophy that teaches the path to happiness, or eudaimonia, is found through living in the moment and not allowing oneself to live in fear of pain or desire for pleasure.

Legalist Ethics

Say Hello to Big Brother

Legalism holds that people are far too swayed by material desires to be guided by a sense of shame, and therefore virtue will not be a motivator of people's actions; instead, clear edicts and strong punishments must be used.

Feminist ethics aims to "understand, criticize, and correct" the binary view of gender, male privilege, and how gender has been used as a construct to maintain oppression.
