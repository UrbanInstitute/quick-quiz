Legalist Ethics: A Summary

Say Hello to Big Brother

Legalism was originally founded based on a response to a particular section in the Analects, a writing of Confucius, that claimed that people must be guided by virtue so that if they fall out of line, a sense of shame will put them back in their place as well as give them a chance for reformation. The most prominent Legalist text, the Hanfeizi, disagrees with this, however. This writing claims that people are too invested in material goods to be led to decisions out of fear of shame. The common people must be guided by clear edicts and strong punishments, and rulers must lead their nations with an iron fist, careful not to be manipulated by their subordinates. The majority of people will always be mediocre in terms of their virtue, and therefore, this philosophy is designed to address that, rather than try to idealize the common people.
